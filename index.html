Prototype position

Built for careful hardware bring-up

Standalone logging

Target firmware records hiking data locally to MicroSD instead of depending completely on a phone.

Module-first testing

ESP32 boot, GPS UART, BMP390L I2C and MicroSD SPI are validated step by step before integration.

Honest status

This is not a finished product. It is a technical prototype for learning, validation and product exploration.

Firmware target

ESP32, GPS, BMP390L and MicroSD

The firmware prototype focuses on readable embedded code, predictable wiring, CSV track logs and practical status feedback through LEDs and a start/stop button.

GPS
NEO-6M compatible module over UART2
Altitude
BMP390L pressure sensor over I2C
Storage
CSV logs on MicroSD over SPI
Controls
GPIO LEDs and INPUT_PULLUP button

Roadmap

Next engineering steps

  1. Repeat GPS validation on the target SoloTrail UART2 pins.
  2. Finalize CSV logging format and integrated firmware tests.
  3. Document real hardware test logs, photos and serial output.
  4. Move from breadboard wiring toward PCB, battery and enclosure validation.

SoloTrail is an early-stage technical prototype, not a certified navigation or safety device.
